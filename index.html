About us

Abstract:

Recent progress from Amazon Picking/Robotics Challenges advanced picking from a cluttered scene, but the problems of object placing are not much addressed. Pose-aware placing is useful in many scenarios for human and machine, e.g., a brandname facing outward on a shelf read by human customers, or a barcode facing up on a conveyer read by a machine scanner. Nevertheless, the challenges remain due to the occlusions among objects in cluttered scenes, which fail many existing object-based segmentation and pose estimation methods. We develop a pose-aware object placing system by spotting the brandname or barcode of objects in a cluttered tote, and then carrying out a sequence of actions placing the objects with desired poses on the shelves or conveyors. We present 1) the brandname-based fully convolutional network (FCN) could predict object pose and affordance and better perform pose-aware placing; 2) active manipulation with two cooperative manipulators and grippers could handle occlusions of brandname that are difficult to achieve with just one. 3) an open benchmark dataset of object, brandname, and barcode in multi-view segmentation is provided for evaluations. Analysis of success rates and failed cases are carried out and discussed for future applications.

Developments

Our Vehicle

NCTU was awarded the WAM-V to the 2018 RobotX Challenge by Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International (AUVSI). This year, we made a Push Goal from "Together We Go Far" to "Together We Go RobotX". We are excited about the coming of WAM-V, and building a highly intelligent system to solve the real-world problem for USV(Unmanned Surface Vehicles).



Our Training Course

We offer a course covering the fundamental and advanced domains in vision for mobility tasks, including the challenges combining object recognition, pose estimation, motion planning, and SLAM. We will systematically study each components from previous winning teams in 2014 and 2016, as well as cutting-edge methods that may better improve the performance. We will use "Duckietown" (an open course "MIT 2.166 Autonomous Vehicles) as platform, and focus advanced topics using Gazebo, 3D perception, and deep learning.



Our Water Access

The Team NCTU will use the in-campus "Bamboo Lake" as the home of the WAM-V. We wish to make RobotX a landmark in NCTU, and therefore we will work together with students and experts who have design backgrounds. We will host a workshop aiming at forming the combination of technology and art and build a competition obsticles that's not only functional and also a piece of art work. By this, we hope to create the value of cross-domain knowledge and creativities.
